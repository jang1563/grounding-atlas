Read before writing any code, writeup, or application text. These come from a rigorous prior-art sweep (full version: `Application_strategy/2026_01_25/RS_Life_Sciences/Novelty_PriorArt_Capability_2026_06_08.md`).

## Novelty: what to CLAIM (defensible, mine)
1. A **content-grounding measurement instrument** (axis B: probe-vs-LLM + LLM-activation probe + content-sensitivity, matched controls). Cross-representation is the design goal; measured so far on protein (FRT is protein-only). No competitor measures content-vs-name grounding quality per representation as a unified instrument.
2. The **reliability-vs-relevance over-trust dissociation** (axis D) as a quantity. Currently exploratory (kappa ~0.36); needs a human-rater pass before it is a claim.
3. A **deterministic, non-LLM-judge eval method** (LabCraft). Valuable, but deterministic-eval alone no longer differentiates (VirBench is deterministic too); the differentiator is applying it to *content-grounding*, not the determinism itself.
4. **NullAtlas as a verifiable-training-signal substrate** (the signal-generation method), the WS2 engine. The general publication-bias finding is now crowding (2606.04220), so make the *substrate* load-bearing, not the general claim, and cite its negative-evidence-coverage result as NullAtlas's, not the grounding instrument's.
5. Multi-omics / single-cell / spatial fluency as the **credible-translator** bridge (positioning).

## Novelty: what NOT to claim (would be scooped) + cite instead
- **Do NOT** claim "build a multimodal bio-LLM that grounds representations" - scooped 15+ ways (NatureLM 2502.07527, Intern-S1 2508.15763, Prot2Chat 2502.06846, Mol-LLaMA 2502.13449, EvoLlama, ProteinGPT, scMMGPT...). Build on it.
- **Do NOT** claim "ground sequences as a modality like vision/pixels" as a novel thesis - the framing is scooped (PixelWorld 2501.19339, NEO 2510.14979 on the vision side; NatureLM/Evo2 on the bio side). **Use the pixel analogy only as exposition.**
- **Do NOT** claim "decide empirically weights vs retrieve vs tools" as a novel method - scooped in general LLM literature. **Cite Ovadia "Fine-Tuning or Retrieval?" (2312.05934) and "Provable Benefits of In-Tool Learning" (2508.20755) and the when-to-retrieve cluster (Adapt-LLM 2404.19705, SeaKR 2406.19215).** Claim only the bio-grounding-specific, cross-representation specialization.
- **Do NOT** claim "add reliability gating to an SFM orchestrator" - partially scooped (Mozi 2603.03655, SciBORG 2507.00081, GeneAgent 2405.16205). Claim the *measurement of trust-calibration on SFM outputs*, not the gating.
- **Do NOT** say "LLMs cannot ground numeric output" as a clean capability gap. For the *numeric* case, reframe as a **verbalization/calibration gap**: the magnitude IS internally encoded ("LLMs Know More About Numbers than They Can Say" 2602.07812, and 2603.02913), with evidence in the LLM's own activations. **Do NOT extend that reading to the NMSE AUROC-0.981 result** - that probe is on ESM-2 (an SFM), not the LLM, so it says nothing about the LLM's internal access. Keep the two separate: numeric = LLM-internal verbalization gap; NMSE = SFM-separable, LLM-side unmeasured, encoding-vs-expression TBD. Conflating them is an easy correction to eat.

## Positioning (the fit argument)
- The adjacent layers filled in during June 2026. **BioMysteryBench** = task solve-rate through tools (orchestrate). **gget virus / VirBench** (Anthropic, 2026-06-08) = agent *retrieval* accuracy on viral queries vs deterministic ground truth. Neither measures content-grounding (content vs name) or trust-calibration on the output. Position as the content-grounding layer in the chain **retrieval (VirBench) -> content-grounding (this) -> downstream**, complementary to both, not a competitor.
- **publication-bias-in-AI** is a second crowding space ("Dead Science Walking" 2606.04220, 2026-06-02). That is the negative-evidence territory NullAtlas occupies as a substrate; lead with the content-grounding instrument (axis B), and cite NullAtlas's result rather than presenting it as this instrument's.
- Eric Kauderer-Abrams's stated obstacle ("no single unambiguous source of truth for the training signal," and he is acknowledged on the VirBench work) is what the WS2 verifiable-signal substrate answers.

## Literature map and differentiation (2026-06 scan)
A targeted 2025-2026 scan of the content-grounding space. The *finding* that LLMs lean on name/context over sequence content is no longer novel; the *instrument* and the activation-probe claims are. Prior art and how we differ:
- **CoKE / "Lost in Tokenization" (2510.23127):** context-only beats sequence+context; raw sequence acts as "informational noise." Behavioral output-quality, protein-focused; **no scramble control, no activation probe, no probe-vs-LLM**. It is our motivation (name over content), not our instrument. Differentiate: we measure *why* (encoding vs expression, mechanistic), not just *that* context helps.
- **GenomeQA (2604.05774):** DNA-only, 6 tasks; a dinucleotide-preserving shuffle on ONE task (splice site); failure modes (motif/composition over-reliance). **No activation probe, no probe-vs-LLM; its CTCF "direct vs indirect" is question phrasing, not same-entity real-vs-corrupted content.** Differentiate: cross-modality (protein + DNA + SMILES + SFM-embedding), same-entity real-vs-corrupted matched contrast, and activation probing. Do NOT let it read as "GenomeQA for protein."
- **Rethinking Text-based Protein Understanding (2505.20354):** retrieval beats LLMs, label leakage >50%. Supports the premise; not an instrument.
- **Inside-Out: Hidden Factual Knowledge (2503.15299):** the canonical encoding-vs-expression (internal vs external knowledge) framework. **General factual QA only.** ADOPT and extend it to biological representations; cite as ancestor, claim the biological extension.
- **Masked by Consensus (2604.12373):** self-LLM-probe vs peer-LLM-probe; factual QA + math; **zero biology, no foundation-model embedding** (it names "embedding model" as a possible external probe but never runs one). Closest method template. Differentiate: ours is an **SFM-embedding probe (ESM-2) vs the LLM's own readout on identical biological inputs**. Borrow its probe design (linear + MLP, every-5th-layer + final, AUC, disagreement subsets).
- **Do Activation Verbalization Methods... (2509.13316):** verbalizing activations through a second LLM reflects the verbalizer's knowledge; **use a simple linear probe, not an LLM verbalizer**, for the activation read-out.
- **InterPLM (2412.12101):** probes ESM-2's own internals. This is the SFM side we are explicitly distinct from (we probe the *LLM's* activations).
- **Zero-shot molecular property prediction is the O1 result, and it is PRIOR ART.** That a direct-prompted LLM is near-chance at predicting a molecular property from SMILES, while fingerprint+ML reaches ~0.9, is established: GPT-4o SMILES-only ROC-AUC 0.4991 across MoleculeNet (2501.13824); GPT embeddings underperform Morgan FP on scaffold splits, FP ~0.88-0.90 ("Can LLMs Understand Molecules" 2402.00024); LLMs fail basic SMILES parsing (CLEANMOL 2505.16340). **Cite these for the gap; do NOT claim the "LLM can't read SMILES property" finding as new.** Scope the claim to **zero-shot, no fine-tuning** (fine-tuned GPT-3 is competitive in low-data, Jablonka NMI 2024). What is still ours: (i) the hERG instantiation as a clean direct-prompt-vs-FP-probe, scaffold-split head-to-head (no paper does this on hERG); (ii) the **validity-vs-property dissociation (O2): the model detects invalid/scrambled SMILES but cannot read the property of valid ones** - not stated anywhere (nearest: CLEANMOL parsing-vs-knowledge, AMORE robustness); (iii) the instrument and the activation arm. Lead with O2 as the conceptual hook, use O1's numbers as evidence.

Open and ours to claim: (1) the cross-modality content-vs-name grounding **instrument** with matched real-vs-corrupted controls; (2) **encoding-vs-expression on the LLM's own activations over biological representations** (extend Inside-Out); (3) the **SFM-embedding probe vs LLM head-to-head** in biology. The SFM-embedding-as-LLM-input variant (scELMo / BioVERSE-style) is the widest-open and not yet in the roadmap.
