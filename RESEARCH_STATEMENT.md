# Research Statement

**JangKeun Kim. For the Life Science Research Scientist role.**

## A grounded biologist, drawn by measurement not assertion

The long-term aim I want to contribute to is Claude as a grounded biologist: a model that performs novel discovery by orchestrating specialist foundation models (ESM, AlphaFold, AlphaMissense, Evo, single-cell models) rather than trying to replace them. The central architectural question is where each capability should live, trained into the model's weights, retrieved, or delegated to an orchestrated specialist call. Today that line is drawn largely by intuition. The preliminary work below builds the instrument that draws it by evidence.

## Discovery is exactly where grounding breaks

Discovery means novel, and novel means absent from web text. I find that a model's ability to ground biological content scales with how often that content's "representation to property" mapping appears in pretraining text, a web-exposure law. The consequence is sharp: grounding is weakest precisely in the discovery regime, where there is nothing left to recall.

I measure this with one three-arm instrument that decomposes grounding into encoding (does the model represent the property internally, via a linear probe on hidden states) and expression (does it state the property, via generation), both against a specialist ceiling (the same property read by a specialist foundation model). Across three modalities, under leakage-controlled splits with selectivity and few-shot supervision controls:

- **Small molecules (SMILES, hERG cardiotoxicity):** a linear probe recovers about 88% of the fingerprint ceiling from the model's hidden states (a notation-invariant structural signal that survives randomized SMILES) yet the model outputs at chance. The bottleneck is expression, not encoding, and it does not close with scale, architecture, alignment, or chain-of-thought reasoning (confirmed across five open-weight models).
- **Proteins (thermostability):** internal encoding itself weakens, to about 55% of the ESM ceiling; the model grounds the property by the source organism name and falls to chance on novel or engineered sequences that carry no name.
- **Variants (clinical pathogenicity):** the web-rich symbolic form (gene plus HGVS) scores 0.79, but largely by clinical-label recall (it falls to 0.61 on experimental DMS labels for the same genes, and a scrambled gene name drops it toward chance). A variant whose symbol the model has not memorized, such as a novel spaceflight variant absent from ClinVar, falls back to the web-poor raw-sequence reading near chance (0.58) while the specialist reads the sequence at 0.96 (and fully unsupervised ESM-1v at 0.92) on post-cutoff variants.

The law operates within a single entity, not only across modalities. The same variant scored as a web-rich symbol (gene plus HGVS, 0.79) versus a web-poor notation (the raw sequence, 0.58) shows that changing only the notation, with the content held fixed, changes whether the model grounds it. This is the general-capability form of a representation-resolution gap I first measured in a safety setting, where an entity was recognized by name at about 100% but by its database accession at about 2%. The shape of the gap tracks web exposure as predicted, cleanest within a single entity where the ceiling is held fixed (a coarse cross-modality web-count proxy did not fit, so this is a qualitative law for now, not a regression), giving a first cross-modality and cross-notation map of where a general model can and cannot ground biological content.

## From measurement to an orchestration map

The decomposition is directly actionable for the architecture question. An expression-limited capability (encoded but not verbalized) is closable by training the read-out. An encoding-limited or genuinely novel capability must be delegated to the specialist, because the model cannot synthesize a signal it never formed. A web-rich recall signal is unsafe to retrieve for novel cases, where it silently fails. This is the grounded-orchestrator design map, measured rather than asserted, and the same instrument doubles as a grounding-and-calibration evaluation that can run on every model release to predict where a model will quietly mis-ground a scientific tool's output.

The representation-invariance failures the map exposes also point to a concrete fix. When the same entity is grounded in one notation but not another, the input form can be normalized before the model reads it, any SMILES to its canonical string, an accession or HGVS to its sequence, by delegating that step to a specialist or tool, and where the content is genuinely encoded but tied to one surface form, a representation-invariant read-out can be trained to bind an entity's notations together. The instrument is what says which failures close by training and which need this orchestration, so the eval and the fix are the same ruler.

## Why this intersection, and what I would build

My background sits on both axes this work needs. Space-biology genomics (GeneLab, single-cell and spatial omics) supplies the novel-discovery cases where grounding matters most, and is the natural source of held-out, never-seen content. My research on over-refusal and calibrated permissioning meets a finding here directly: safety-tuned models refuse the web-poor raw-content form of a query while freely answering the web-rich symbolic form, so grounding and safety can be read on one ruler. Joining the team, I would extend this preliminary work along three lines: the placement map (train, retrieve, or orchestrate) across the full modality ladder, including raw structure and single-cell data; a release-cadence grounding and calibration evaluation; and the safety-grounding intersection that calibrated permissioning for biology depends on.
