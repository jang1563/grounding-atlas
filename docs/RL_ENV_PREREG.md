# PREREG: Route-vs-Train in the Generative Regime

## 1. Title and framing

**Route-vs-Train in the Generative Regime: a contamination-safe 3-arm head-to-head (internalized reward-update vs frozen-FM inference-time guidance vs base) on a SMILES generator with our hERG ADMET head as the reward.**

Our `route, don't train` verdict (`docs/REPORT.md`, and the `decision-map-placement` line) was established entirely on the **read-out / property-prediction** lever: across 17+ cells we showed that retrieve (knowledge / in-data-pattern) plus orchestrate (specialist / canonicalize / compute) cover every measured cell and the *weights* lever wins nowhere, including the LoRA confirmations (`results/ws3_lora_cells.json`) where a trained head was always a strong-second to an orchestrated frozen specialist. That entire program is **discriminative**: it asks whether training the weights helps you *score* an input better. It never tested the one place the AI-safety literature says weight-updates are supposed to matter most: **post-training alignment to elicit emergent capability in the generative regime** — RL / preference-optimization that internalizes a reward into a *generative* policy's weights to make it *produce* new artifacts, not merely classify existing ones. This pre-registration tests exactly that lever and nothing else. It asks whether INTERNALIZING our calibrated hERG specialist (the same `orchestrate` move) into a frozen SMILES generator's weights produces more genuine, oracle-confirmed design success than STEERING the same frozen generator with the same specialist at inference time, at equal budget. A clean answer either OVERTURNS `route, don't train` (the generative/RL lever is the exception the read-out work could not see) or EXTENDS it into generation (even when you let the weights move and let the model *generate*, routing a frozen prior through a specialist still wins). This is the generative/RL lever, explicitly NOT the read-out lever we already ran.

## 2. The one-line question

> On ONE frozen molecular SMILES generator, with our trained hERG property head AS the reward, does INTERNALIZING that reward into the policy's weights (on-policy PPO primary; g-DPO as a budget-feasible alternate) produce more held-out-**oracle** design success — sampled novel molecules clearing a fitness bar on an oracle *disjoint* from the reward — than STEERING the same frozen generator with the same reward at inference (Best-of-N / rerank) at EQUAL reward-query budget? I.e. does the RL lever OVERTURN or merely EXTEND `route, don't train` into generation?

Framing: **internalized-update (train) vs frozen-FM-plus-inference-time-control (route)**, generator-vs-generator, judged only on a contamination-safe held-out oracle.

## 3. Background grounding

- **RLXF (bioRxiv 2025.05.02.651993)** — on-policy RL of a frozen generative protein/sequence FM against a learned reward, with a KL leash to a frozen reference, *does* move a generative policy toward a reward. Licenses arm A's shape (on-policy rollouts + frozen reference + KL) and makes the KL coefficient load-bearing, not cosmetic.
- **VIDD (arXiv 2507.00445)** — value-weighted iterative distillation internalizes a reward via *weighted* likelihood. Licenses a g-DPO / value-weighted alternate for arm A, but its weighted-CE form is precisely why we must guard it against collapsing into supervised imitation (it is mechanically close to weighted MLE).
- **g-DPO (arXiv 2510.19474)** — reward-ranked preference optimization for molecular generation; a CE-style pairwise loss that reuses a classifier-style training loop. Licenses g-DPO as the A40-feasible internalization optimizer, while flagging that "reward in the loss" is necessary-not-sufficient.
- **ProteinGuide (arXiv 2505.04823 / 2505.15093)** — inference-time guidance of a frozen generative FM with a property predictor (re-weighting the frozen prior toward the reward) is competitive with, and in low-data regimes beats, weight-internalization. Licenses arm B as a first-class comparator and motivates the low-data regime where guidance is predicted to win.
- **Ferruz survey (arXiv 2511.21476)** — surveys generative protein design and warns that (a) reward-model reliability bounds any RL claim, and (b) frozen-prior steering "re-weights but does not extrapolate." Licenses the reward-reliability battery (H3) and the elicit-vs-reweight mechanism arm (H4); its strong "RL never expands the frontier" claim was refuted 0-3 in our prior deep research, so H4 is genuinely OPEN.

## 4. Design

### 4.1 Frozen FM choice (and fallback)

**v1 primary substrate (REVISED per build + truefitness critics):** a frozen, **UNCONDITIONAL** small SMILES language model — either (i) an off-the-shelf pretrained ChEMBL/ZINC SMILES LM (~10-100M params) **with an auditable, disclosed pretraining SMILES set**, or (ii) a tiny char/BPE SMILES LM (1-5M params) **trained by us** on a disclosed, scaffold-controlled subset of the in-repo ADMET corpus (`signal/admet/*/pairs.jsonl`, condition=='matched'), <1 A40-GPU-hour. We **drop the ChemBERTa-conditioned decoder fiction**: `eval/smiles_generator_init.py` does not exist, no autoregressive decoder exists in the repo, and conditioning on a 384-dim latent is brand-new high-risk infra not needed for a route-vs-train verdict. The generator autoregressively `.sample()`s, every draw is RDKit-validated, canonicalized, de-duplicated. Frozen weights are the shared substrate for all 3 arms; only arm A updates a LoRA-wrapped policy copy. **Note on the conflict (build vs original design):** we side with the build critic — an externally-pretrained, *disclosed-corpus* generator is preferred over a conditioned in-repo one, because (a) it makes the contamination/novelty checks auditable, and (b) it frees the in-corpus hERG molecules for the reward/oracle partition (Section 4.3) instead of spending them on generator pretraining. If we self-train (option ii), its training scaffolds become a named partition block and are excluded from the novelty/oracle blocks.

**Fallback (v2-deferred, do NOT run in v1):** PROTEIN (ESM-2 / EvoDiff + AlphaFold-Multimer ipTM / docking oracle). BLOCKED in v1 by a hard data gap — the repo has no wet-lab protein ADMET targets, hence no trustworthy protein reward or held-out oracle. The molecular loop is decisive on its own.

### 4.2 Reward (and the independent held-out oracle)

**Reward head (must be BUILT, not unpickled — `orchestrate_arm.json` is metrics-only):** re-fit the balanced-LR head over frozen ChemBERTa-77M-MTR 384-dim embeddings from `signal/sfm_embedding/chemberta_herg.npz` (and `chemberta_clearance.npz` for the degraded regime), **serialize to a real `.pkl`**, and assert reproduction of within-AUROC 0.8667. Write the missing `eval/reward_head_load.py` + `eval/sfm_embed_chemberta.py` wiring so `reward(smiles) -> P` is callable. `reward(smiles)` = embed (ChemBERTa forward pass) -> `predict_proba` -> P(desired hERG class). The ChemBERTa embedder is the real per-sample reward cost and must co-reside on the A40; cache embeddings of seen canonical SMILES and batch the embedder. The same head + embedder are used **identically** across arms A and B so reward quality cancels in the paired contrast.

**Reward is an ENSEMBLE with a pessimistic penalty (per reward critic):** reward = `mean(P) - lambda * std(P)` over a 5-member ensemble (bootstrap-resampled LR heads / mixed featurizations), with `lambda` pre-registered (default 1.0). The single-head reward is retained as a **named ablation** so the ensemble's effect on the reward-hacking gap is itself a measured result. This converts the design from "detect hacking after the fact" to "reward designed to resist hacking."

**OOD reward gate (per reward critic, MANDATORY):** the LR/RF head over a frozen embedding has no off-manifold calibration guarantee, and PPO will push into low-density corners. Before the head-to-head we (a) measure reward reliability **on FM-generated candidates** (calibration curve of reward vs oracle on *samples*, plus reward stratified by k-NN Tanimoto / Mahalanobis embedding-distance to the head's training support), and (b) **gate the reward**: clip / down-weight P when the candidate's embedding is OOD to the head's training support. The gate is pre-registered. If the head is uncalibrated beyond support (likely), the un-gated raw reward arm is VOID; only the gated reward runs.

**Held-out oracle (the verdict scorer; MANDATORY, causally independent):** a Random-Forest / GBT on Morgan/ECFP4 fingerprints, **different model class AND different featurization AND scaffold-disjoint** from the reward head and the generator. Independence language is **downgraded from "causally independent" to "reduced-leakage"** (per validity + truefitness critics): Morgan and ChemBERTa both read the same 2D substructure surface and both oracle and reward draw from the same hERG label lineage, so feature-level and label-level leakage are only partially broken. We therefore **promote QuickVina2 docking (a 3D-physics, mechanistically independent surface) from v2 into v1 as a co-primary oracle**; OVERTURN claims require **orthogonal-oracle confirmation** (Morgan-RF agreeing with docking). Where an external-assay hERG set is stageable, the Morgan oracle's labels are sourced from a **different assay/lineage** than the reward head's `negres_admet` labels to break shared label noise; if not stageable, the shared-lineage dependency is disclosed as a binding limitation. The oracle is used ONLY as a per-molecule pass/fail labeler on sampled novel designs to compute a success-RATE; **its own AUROC is never an arm outcome.** The reward-head AUROC (0.8667) may appear ONLY in a reward-model-diagnostic appendix.

### 4.3 The three arms (+ named control D)

| Arm | Kind | What it is | Binding invariant |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A internalized reward-update** | train | **On-policy PPO (PRIMARY, required):** LoRA-wrapped policy copy of the frozen generator; reward = ensemble head on the policy's OWN freshly-sampled rollouts; advantage = reward - baseline; **frozen reference + pre-registered KL leash.** Alternate (budget-feasible, see build critic): **g-DPO** over preference pairs **derived from the policy's own current samples** (not a fixed offline high-label set). | Loss MUST contain reward as advantage/log-ratio/value-weight; gradient provably depends on `reward(sample)`. Reward-shuffle ablation MUST collapse A toward base (else supervised -> VOID). g-DPO/VIDD additionally require on-policy-sample-derived pairs (else it is arm D in disguise -> VOID). |
| **B external guidance** | orchestrate | Inference-time steering of the FROZEN generator with the IDENTICAL ensemble reward + IDENTICAL embedder + IDENTICAL budget. **v1 tiers: (B0) Best-of-N** (sample N, keep top-reward) and **(B-rerank)** generate-then-rerank/filter — the direct generative lift of our discriminative orchestrate winner. **N is SWEPT** (scaling curve). | Every candidate B ranks must itself be a freshly SAMPLED, RDKit-valid, not-in-train+pool string (novelty/uniqueness asserted) — else B-rerank is a classifier on a fixed pool -> VOID. |
| **C base FM** | baseline | Un-steered frozen generator (do-nothing prior). | Bounds whether either optimizing arm escapes the pretraining support; "beats C" is necessary-not-sufficient. |
| **D supervised-imitation** | named control | CE / MLE fine-tune on high-reward-label SMILES, NO reward in the loss. | Present so a CE-only run can never be mislabeled as arm A. If used as an SFT warm-start, it MUST be followed by a measured reward-driven update. |

**Conflict note (build vs drift on arm A optimizer):** the drift critic demands on-policy PPO be PRIMARY (g-DPO risks being weighted-MLE); the build critic demands g-DPO PRIMARY (3-model PPO co-residence OOMs on 40GB with an 8B base). We resolve in favor of a clean test **by removing the cause of the conflict**: with the **small (<100M) SMILES generator** mandated in 4.1 (not an 8B base), base + LoRA-policy + frozen-reference + reward-embedder + RF-oracle co-reside comfortably, so **on-policy PPO IS the primary arm A** as drift requires. g-DPO is retained as a budget-feasible alternate that must pass BOTH the reward-shuffle-collapse AND the on-policy-derived-pairs asserts. SMC / Feynman-Kac guidance (per-step particle re-weighting) is **deferred to v2** (build critic: high implementation risk, not needed for the H1 verdict; Best-of-N with swept N already gives the scaling curve).

### 4.4 Regimes

1. **reward_data_size (PIVOTAL):** number of hERG labels training the reward head and/or the rollout/pair budget for A. Levels: **low (~150 labels)** vs **full (~3963)**. Locate the crossover; the winner is expected to flip with size. **Caveat (truefitness):** ~150 labels at 15.8% positive is ~24 positives — both head and oracle are high-variance here; the low-data cell carries a pre-registered underpowered-no-verdict gate (Section 7).
2. **reward_quality_noise:** STRONG hERG head (0.8667) vs DEGRADED clearance head (0.6424) or injected label noise. Prediction: internalized RL Goodharts the noisier surface harder (larger train-reward-minus-oracle gap); guidance re-queries the frozen prior and is more robust.

Regimes 3 (objective_count / multi-objective) and 4 (extended novelty-radius de-novo) from the original design are **moved to v2** (build critic: multi-objective + SMC are the bulk of the new-infra risk). v1 is single-objective; H4 (elicit-vs-reweight) is tested at the in-support -> held-out-scaffold radius only.

## 5. Drift guards (numbered invariants — the heart of the doc)

1. **GENERATIVE-SUCCESS-NOT-DISCRIMINATOR-AUROC.** Headline = success-rate@oracle-threshold / novelty-at-quality over DRAWN samples. The reward head's AUROC/Spearman appears ONLY in a diagnostic appendix. `compare_rl_orchestrate.py` carries an **executable assert that FAILS the run if the headline metric is an AUROC.** Headline is a discriminator AUROC -> VOID.
2. **REWARD+POLICY-NOT-CE-ON-LABELS.** Arm A's loss MUST contain reward as advantage/log-ratio/value-weight; assert gradient depends on `reward(sample)`. On-policy PPO is the primary, required form. Reward-shuffle ablation MUST collapse A to base. g-DPO/VIDD additionally require on-policy-sample-derived pairs. "Reward in the loss" is explicitly necessary-not-sufficient. Survives reward-shuffle, or pairs are offline-fixed -> VOID.
3. **HELD-OUT-ORACLE-NOT-TRAINING-REWARD.** Final designs judged ONLY by the held-out oracle (different model class + reduced-leakage features + scaffold-disjoint, with docking as the orthogonal co-primary). Train-reward-minus-oracle gap reported first-class. reward==oracle (no independent fitness) -> VOID.
4. **EXTERNAL-GENERATIVE-GUIDANCE-NOT-A-DISCRIMINATIVE-HEAD.** The load-bearing comparator is internalized-update vs frozen-FM + inference-time SAMPLING-control (Best-of-N / rerank) on the IDENTICAL reward and budget. A discriminative head that generates nothing is NOT a comparator. Steering arm absent or under-budget -> VOID.
5. **MUST-ACTUALLY-SAMPLE (bound to A, B, B-rerank, C).** Require autoregressive sampling; persist generated strings; assert NOT in train/pool **and not in the generator's actual pretraining corpus** (Section 6). If >X% (pre-registered, default 5%) of any arm's "designs" trace to pre-existing pool/pretraining rows, that arm is a re-ranker not a generator -> VOID.
6. **COMPUTE-MATCHED-STEERING ON A PRE-REGISTERED AXIS.** Primary budget axis = **total reward-model queries** (the scarce resource the question is framed on); secondary robustness panel = **total generator FLOPs (forward + backward)**. Log forward-pass, backward-pass, and reward-query counters per arm and persist them. B's N is swept across >= one decade so the curve brackets A's budget on BOTH axes. "A wins" counts ONLY vs the BEST compute-matched B point AND must survive both accountings. Single-point or under-budget steering -> VOID.
7. **REWARD-QUALITY-NOT-MISLABELED-AS-LEVER.** Hold reward FIXED across arms; run the head-to-head across >= 2 reward-quality/data-size regimes; report the LEVER effect as the within-reward paired difference; every claim is "at THIS reward quality, lever X wins," never unqualified.
8. **REWARD-RELIABILITY-ON-GENERATIONS (new, per reward critic).** Reward reliability is measured on FM-generated candidates (calibration vs oracle on samples + OOD-stratified), not assumed from the in-distribution discriminative AUROC. An OOD reward gate is pre-registered and applied. Un-gated raw reward beyond training support -> VOID. The reward-shuffle ablation is explicitly NOT counted as evidence of reward reliability.
9. **KL-LEASH IS COMMITTED (new, per reward + validity critics).** A pre-registered KL coefficient (swept `{0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5}`, selected on a HELD-OUT, not training-reward, criterion) with KL-to-base reported for every arm-A result. An A-win achievable only at near-zero KL (abandoning the frozen prior) is reported as drift, not a lever win. A "KL-only / reward-zeroed-but-entropy-tuned" reference is included so prior-sharpening is separable from reward-driven gain.

## 6. Contamination-safe true-fitness evaluation

**Endpoint name (per truefitness critic):** "held-out **in-silico** oracle agreement," NOT "true fitness." With no wet lab the oracle is a contamination-controlled surrogate; **no claim of real molecular fitness is made**, and any OVERTURN is provisional pending orthogonal (docking) confirmation.

**Explicit 4-way scaffold partition (resolves the original contradictory disjointness rule).** The original design required the oracle to be disjoint from the generator's corpus while defining the generator's corpus as the full hERG set — unsatisfiable. We pre-register an EXPLICIT Murcko-scaffold partition of the hERG corpus into four mutually disjoint blocks, asserted with hard failure in `eval/build_holdout_oracle.py`:
- **block-R** — reward-head training scaffolds
- **block-G** — generator conditioning/finetune scaffolds (EMPTY if we use an external-pretrained generator; this is the strong reason to prefer one)
- **block-O** — oracle training scaffolds
- **block-E** — the "novel success" eval scaffolds

`block-R ∩ block-G ∩ block-O ∩ block-E == empty` is asserted in code; the generator is demonstrably never trained on block-O or block-E. A **feasibility script runs FIRST** and prints per-block molecule and POSITIVE counts on the ~3963-mol / 625-pos / 1773-scaffold hERG corpus. We pre-register a **minimum RF-train positive count and a minimum valid-novel-success count**; if the 3-way carve starves a block (expected ~150-200 oracle positives), we relax to a 2-way reward-vs-oracle split with the **oracle drawn from an external hERG dataset** (freeing in-corpus positives), and/or **pool ames (0.8812, 414 pos) as a second strong-reward endpoint** so the verdict does not hinge on one starved corpus. Block-O positive count is reported as a reliability covariate per cell.

**Two-layer novelty hold-out.** (1) Scaffold split: success is counted only on block-E scaffolds, disjoint from block-R/G/O. (2) Designs asserted absent from train+pool **AND from the generator's ACTUAL pretraining corpus** (full canonical-SMILES set), with nearest-neighbor Tanimoto to that full set reported — not just the local 22k pool (truefitness blocker: a string novel to the local pool but present in the generator's millions-of-SMILES pretraining set is a memorization leak). If the pretraining set is an unauditable off-the-shelf checkpoint, we either self-train a disclosed-corpus generator or restrict success-counting to beyond-pretraining-support regions and down-weight in-support "success" as possibly-memorized.

**Reward-hacking check (first-class).** Report train-reward-minus-oracle GAP per arm. Hacking signature = training-reward UP but held-out-oracle success FLAT/DOWN -> CONFIRMS route-don't-train. Because oracle and reward share a 2D substructure surface, the gap is a **lower bound** on true hacking; the docking co-primary detects shared-surface hacking the Morgan oracle cannot. Additional hacking tells: fraction of high-reward designs falling **outside training-corpus property bounds** (QED/SA-score/heavy-atom/ring-count ranges) reported independent of the oracle.

**Capability vs sampling-efficiency (per truefitness critic).** Success-RATE alone can be raised by distribution-sharpening. We add as **co-primary**: **pass@k (k swept)** and **unique-valid-novel-above-bar COUNT at FIXED total sample budget (coverage)**, and report the oracle-success-vs-N scaling curve for ALL arms (not just B) so sharpening (curve shifts left, same asymptote) is visually separable from capability (asymptote rises). **An arm-A win counts as capability ONLY if it raises the COUNT of distinct oracle-passing novel scaffolds at matched budget**, not merely the rate.

**Other metrics:** validity rate (RDKit-parseable), uniqueness rate, internal diversity (mean pairwise Tanimoto distance), nearest-neighbor Tanimoto to training data (novelty radius for H4), B's steering scaling curve, mean training-reward per arm (necessary-not-sufficient sanity), reward-ablation delta (must be large else VOID).

**In-silico caveat (binding).** The oracle is a surrogate of a surrogate; agreement between two correlated in-silico models is not molecular truth. Confirm the Morgan-oracle's label lineage differs from the reward head's `negres_admet`; if identical, disclose the shared-bias dependency as a limitation.

## 7. Pre-registered hypotheses, primary endpoint, and the planned test

**Primary endpoint.** Held-out-oracle **success-rate@pre-committed-fitness-threshold** among **unique-valid-NOVEL** sampled designs (oracle = Morgan-RF, scaffold-disjoint; docking co-primary for OVERTURN), as the **two-sample paired-by-cluster difference: arm A MINUS the BEST compute-matched arm B point.** The fitness bar is a **percentile of the oracle's score distribution on a reference set** (NOT an absolute probability), so the success rate is non-degenerate given the 15.8% hERG positive rate. Co-primary: novelty-at-quality COUNT and pass@k coverage. NOT a discriminator AUROC; NOT the training reward; NOT "beats base."

**Planned statistical test (REVISED — original `paired_cluster_boot` is inapplicable).** The validity critic verified (`eval/probe_common.py:236`) that `paired_cluster_boot` differences TWO AUROCs over a SHARED `(y, p_a, p_b)` item vector with common labels. Arms A and B emit DIFFERENT molecules, so there is no shared item set and no per-item pairing — the pre-wired machinery cannot run on this endpoint. We therefore pre-register a **NEW scaffold-clustered TWO-SAMPLE bootstrap**: cluster-bootstrap the 0/1 oracle-pass indicator by Murcko scaffold WITHIN each arm's generated set, resample arms independently, take the difference of per-arm success-rates, percentile 95% CI on `(rate_A - rate_B)`, `n_boot=1000`. This is written and unit-tested as a new `probe_common` function; the existing Murcko-clustering and bootstrap utilities are reused, the paired-AUROC core is not.

**Arm B operating-point selection (per validity critic):** B's best compute-matched N is frozen on a **validation oracle split disjoint from the final test scaffolds** (or on the reward head) BEFORE test scoring — never selected on the test oracle used for the verdict.

**Decision rule (sharpened — no ambiguous middle zone):**
- **CONFIRM route-don't-train** if the `(A - B)` CI includes 0 or favors B, OR `|Δ| < 0.03` success-rate (tie band, mirrors BRIDGE_3WAY >= 0.03 ordering).
- **OVERTURN** ONLY if the CI **LOWER bound > 0.03** success-rate in A's favor (not just the mean), on the ORACLE, at equal reward-query budget AND surviving FLOP-matching, WITHOUT collapsing novelty/diversity/validity, AND with **orthogonal (docking) confirmation**.
- **Hard numeric collapse gates (per validity critic):** an A-win is VOIDED if validity rate drops > 10%, internal diversity (mean pairwise Tanimoto distance) drops > 15%, or novelty-at-quality count falls below the pre-registered minimum. (X=10, Y=15 pinned now.)
- **UNDERPOWERED-NO-VERDICT** if unique-valid-novel N per arm, or block-O/block-E positive counts, fall below the pre-registered minima (mirrors the spectra_ms near-miss discipline).

**H1_PRIMARY.** At matched reward-query budget and FIXED identical reward, internalized RL (A) does NOT beat the best compute-matched external-guidance point (B) on held-out-oracle success-rate@threshold. *Confirm* (route-don't-train EXTENDS to generation) per the CONFIRM rule; *Overturn* per the OVERTURN rule above.

**H2_regime_flip.** The winner is regime-conditioned: B wins in LOW-data and DEGRADED-reward regimes; A can only win, if ever, with abundant high-quality reward. *Outcome:* report the crossover across regime #1 and #2. An A-win in the strong/full cell that REVERSES in low-data/degraded is NOT a clean overturn -> "lever choice is regime-conditioned."

**H3_reward_hacking.** Any arm-A lift on training reward over-states its oracle lift (positive train-reward-minus-oracle gap), more so under the degraded reward. *Outcome:* a large, reward-reliability-growing gap -> Goodharting -> CONFIRMS route-don't-train. A small, regime-stable gap (and surviving the ensemble-pessimistic reward) is required before any overturn is credible.

**H4_elicit_vs_reweight (OPEN).** Neither arm produces oracle-confirmed novel-yet-true molecules beyond frozen-prior reach (re-weight, not extrapolate); OR if one does, it is the mechanism-level overturn. *Outcome:* if A yields docking-confirmed beyond-support designs (block-E scaffolds) B cannot reach, by the two-sample CI, that is positive evidence for elicitation/extrapolation by weight-update. If both only re-weight within support, route-don't-train holds and Ferruz's "frozen steering cannot extrapolate" is consistent.

## 8. v1 buildable cell, Cayuga sbatch shape, effort; v2-deferred

**v1 numbered steps (build order; contamination self-test gates the grid):**
1. `eval/reward_head_load.py` — re-fit balanced-LR hERG head from `chemberta_herg.npz` (and `chemberta_clearance.npz`), **serialize a real `.pkl`**, assert within-AUROC 0.8667 reproduced; build the 5-member ensemble + pessimistic `mean - lambda*std`; wire `reward(smiles) -> P` via `eval/sfm_embed_chemberta.py` (batched, embedding-cached). CPU + light GPU.
2. `eval/build_holdout_oracle.py` — run the 4-way scaffold-partition **feasibility count FIRST**; assert block-R/G/O/E disjointness with hard failure; train RF/GBT-on-Morgan on block-O; freeze oracle + test_ids; stage QuickVina2 docking co-primary; assert generator never sees block-O/E. Gate the grid behind a "contamination self-test passes" CI flag.
3. `eval/smiles_generator_init.py` — instantiate the frozen UNCONDITIONAL SMILES LM (external-pretrained disclosed-corpus, or self-trained tiny LM <1 GPU-hr); expose real autoregressive `.sample()`; RDKit-validate/canonicalize/de-dup vs train+pool **and vs the generator's full pretraining set**; log validity/uniqueness/novelty. **PRE-RUN POWER GATE:** verify validity, unique-novel yield per 1k, internal diversity clear pre-committed minima AND arm C produces a non-trivial count of oracle-passing molecules — else the cell is "underpowered, no verdict" before any A-vs-B read. This is arm C.
4. `eval/rl_guidance.py` — arm B: Best-of-N (B0) + generate-then-rerank (B-rerank), frozen generator + ensemble reward; sweep N; log forward/reward-query counters; novelty assert on every ranked candidate.
5. `eval/rl_ppo.py` — arm A: LoRA policy copy (reuse `eval/ws3_lora.py` gradient_checkpointing + grad-accum) + **on-policy PPO** (reward = ensemble head on own rollouts, frozen ref + swept KL); assert reward in loss; reward-shuffle ablation as a CI flag; log forward/backward/reward-query + KL-to-base. g-DPO drop-in alternate with on-policy-pair + shuffle asserts.
6. `eval/rl_arm_d_sft.py` — arm D named CE control (no reward in loss).
7. `eval/compare_rl_orchestrate.py` — score ALL arms' SAMPLED designs ONLY on the held-out oracle(s); compute success-rate@percentile-threshold, novelty-at-quality, pass@k/coverage, train-reward-minus-oracle gap; run the **new scaffold-clustered two-sample bootstrap** on `(A - bestB)`; **executable assert that fails the run if the headline metric is an AUROC** and if any arm's designs trace > 5% to pool/pretraining rows; emit `results/rl_route_vs_train.json`. Enforce equal reward-query budget + FLOP-matched panel.
8. Run v1 grid: reward ∈ {hERG-strong, clearance-degraded} × data-size ∈ {low ~150, full} = **4 cells**, single objective, arms A/B/C (+ D control), held-out oracle. Pre-commit thresholds, the >= 0.03 margin (CI-lower-bound rule), collapse gates, and VOID conditions BEFORE running.
9. Write the verdict per overturn/confirm/regime-dependent/underpowered/VOID criteria; report steering scaling curve, oracle-vs-N curves for all arms, and the reward-hacking gap as first-class figures.

## 9. Risks, mitigations, and kill-criteria

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Reward-hacking masquerades as a train win (A exploits the head's blind spots). | Head is the REWARD, never the evaluator; ensemble + pessimistic `mean - lambda*std`; OOD reward gate; train-reward-minus-oracle gap first-class; docking co-primary catches shared-2D-surface hacking; degrade reward as a sweep (H3). |
| Steering arm under-powered so A wins by default. | Compute-matched on pre-registered reward-query axis + FLOP panel; B's N swept with a scaling curve; A wins only vs the BEST compute-matched B point; low-data regime (where B should win) retained as a sanity anchor; single-point B -> VOID. |
| "RL arm" silently reduces to CE/SFT. | On-policy PPO is primary; reward term asserted in loss; reward-shuffle must collapse A to base; g-DPO requires on-policy-derived pairs; CE-only is the NAMED arm D -> VOID otherwise. |
| Primary test inapplicable (paired_cluster_boot needs shared items; arms emit different molecules). | Replaced with a new scaffold-clustered TWO-SAMPLE bootstrap on the per-design oracle indicator; written and unit-tested before the grid. |
| Budget non-equivalence flips the verdict. | Single pre-registered primary axis (reward-model queries) + secondary FLOP-matched panel; three counters logged per arm; A-win must survive both. |
| KL leash uncommitted -> A-win is just off-manifold drift. | KL swept `{0.01,0.05,0.1,0.5}`, selected on held-out criterion, KL-to-base reported every result; KL-only reference separates prior-sharpening from reward-driven gain. |
| Oracle not actually independent (shares 2D surface + label lineage with reward). | Different model class + featurization + scaffold-disjoint + **docking co-primary on a 3D-physics surface**; external-assay labels where stageable; independence language downgraded to "reduced-leakage"; gap reported as a lower bound. |
| Scaffold partition starves denominators (625 pos / 1773 scaffolds). | Feasibility count FIRST; pre-registered minimum RF-train and valid-novel-success counts; relax to 2-way + external oracle or pool ames; underpowered-no-verdict gate. |
| Novelty defined only vs local 22k pool -> pretraining leak counted as success. | De-dup vs the generator's FULL pretraining canonical-SMILES set; NN-Tanimoto to that full set; disclosed-corpus generator preferred; restrict success to beyond-support if checkpoint unauditable. |
| Small generator yields few valid/novel molecules -> tiny denominators, weak B handing A a spurious win. | PRE-RUN POWER GATE on validity/novelty/diversity + arm-C oracle-pass count; percentile fitness bar; underpowered-no-verdict rather than over-reading noise. |
| Reward uncalibrated off-manifold (LR over frozen embedding). | Reward-reliability-on-generations battery (calibration vs oracle on samples, OOD-stratified) + pre-registered OOD gate; un-gated raw reward -> VOID. |
| Flat result across all arms read as failure. | Pre-registered as a legitimate PLACEMENT-AGNOSTIC NEGATIVE — the generative analogue of the BRIDGE_3WAY flat outcome ("don't pay to train the weights"). Reported honestly, not buried. |

**What would make us abandon this (kill-criteria):** abandon the cell (not the program) if the PRE-RUN POWER GATE fails (generator cannot emit enough valid-novel-diverse molecules, or arm C clears too few oracle bars) OR the 4-way scaffold feasibility count cannot supply the pre-registered minimum block-O/block-E positive counts even after relaxing to an external-assay oracle and pooling ames; in that case the molecular substrate cannot power a generative route-vs-train test and we report "underpowered, no verdict" rather than reading noise. Abandon the broader claim only if, additionally, the protein fallback's data gap remains unfilled — i.e. no buildable generative oracle exists in either domain.
